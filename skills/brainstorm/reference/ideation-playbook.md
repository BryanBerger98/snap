# Ideation playbook — diverge, then converge

The mandate: **generate**. A brainstorm where the facilitator only writes down the
user's ideas has failed. Push the list wide first; filter second.

## Divergence angles (run each, blind to the others)
- **Pain × persona matrix** — take every (persona, pain) cell from the personas and
  force at least one feature idea per cell. The systematic angle; catches gaps.
- **Adjacent jobs** — what does the persona do right *before* and *after* the core job?
  Features often live in the adjacency (setup, hand-off, follow-up).
- **Competitor / category patterns** — what do comparable products ship as table stakes?
  What do they deliberately omit? Use `WebSearch`/`WebFetch` when a quick scan helps; cite
  what you found.
- **Edge & lifecycle** — onboarding, empty states, errors, scale, offline, admin,
  export/delete. The unglamorous features that decide whether the product is usable.
- **Provocations** — "what would the 10× version do?", "what if it were fully automated?"

## Keep it messy
Do not score or cut while generating. Quantity now, quality next. Invite the user to pile
on. A short, tidy, pre-filtered list is the anti-pattern.

## The converge bar (in `converge-features`)
A feature survives only if it serves a **named persona pain**. No pain → cut or park with
a reason. Merge near-duplicates. Each survivor gets a value hypothesis
(`We believe <X>, measured by <Y>`). Prioritization is **not** done here — that's
`/roadmap`.

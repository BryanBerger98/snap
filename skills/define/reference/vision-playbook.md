# Vision & personas playbook — probe bank for `PER-*` + the Brief's vision

Who it's for, why, and how we'll know it worked. Dwell here — this is where the product's
reason to exist gets sharp. Shared posture: `../product-model/discovery.md`.

## Per persona (1–3, kept `proto`)
- **JTBD** — the job to be done, one sentence: "When <situation>, I want to <motivation>,
  so I can <outcome>."
- **Top pains** this product removes (tie each to the Brief's problem).
- **Desired gains** — what a good day looks like once solved.
- **Triggers / context of use** — when, where, device, constraints.
- **2–3 key scenarios** — concrete moments of use.

Challenge: is this a distinct person with a distinct job, or the same persona renamed?
Merge duplicates. No fictional bio (name, age, photo) until `validé`.

## Vision & success (updates `BRF-001`)
- **Why** — restate the reason the product should exist, anchored on the personas.
- **Objective** — what changes for the user when it works.
- **Success metric** — the single measure that tells us it's working (the North Star
  from the Brief, now made concrete and instrumented). How is it measured? What value =
  success? Push back on vanity metrics.

## Quality bar
A persona with no pain tied to the Brief, a "success" that can't be measured, or a vision
that just lists features → keep digging. Restate, confirm, then write the personas and
update the Brief's FAQ (`Pour qui`, `Comment on mesure le succès`).
